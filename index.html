Coda

Early Life

Kazusou Oda (Coda) is a very talented Japanese songwriter, singer, composer, and sound producer. Coda was born in Hiroshima, Japan on January 17, 1980. Coda's love of music started with his father, who was a music teacher, who taught him the basics of muisc. He also expolred the piano, trumpet, recorder, and many other classical intruments.


No Regret Life

Coda began his career by forming the band No Regret Life in January 2001. The band relased their first album Tomorrow Is The Another Day" in November of 2002. The members moved Tokyo in 2005 to further their career.

In 2005 No Regret Life made their first major release "Melody" which was a single. 4 months later the band released their second single "Lost Word," which was used in the 9th ending theme of the Naruto TV series.

Separation

In 2011, No Regret Life left Sony and established their own label under the name Spiral-Motion. At this point, Oda would begin assisting and managing other bands. The band would go on to release 5 full albums, 4 mini albums and 7 singles before their separation in 2013.
